# Community Covenant: Cultural Charter and Contribution Policy

## Preamble: The Mysterium Magnum of Collective Endeavor

This **Community Covenant** serves as the cultural constitution of the **hummingbird_style** repository. It binds all contributors‚Äîhuman practitioners, AI agents, editors, illustrators, researchers, and all other stakeholders‚Äîto shared principles that honor sovereignty, diversity, ethical conduct, and the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom within the domains of bodywork, integrated mystical cultivation, and allied disciplines.

We recognize that knowledge transmission is a sacred act requiring transparency, mutual respect, and accountability across the entire 360-degree purview of this repository's lifecycle: from ideation and authorship through publication, usage, and archival stewardship.

This document subsumes and extends:
- The repository's [Statement of Disclaimer](./DISCLAIMER.md)
- [Versioning and Quality Control](./VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md) standards
- The [GNU General Public License v3](./LICENSE)
- Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Belonging (DEIB) commitments
- Standards for human and AI-agent collaboration

---

## I. Foundational Philosophy and Mission

### 1.1 Repository Mission

The **hummingbird_style** repository serves as:
- A living treasury of bodywork traditions and integrated mystical practices
- An educational resource grounded in transparency and documented provenance
- A sanctuary for cross-cultural wisdom that respects lineage and attribution
- A laboratory for human-AI collaborative knowledge stewardship

### 1.2 Guiding Principles

All participants in this repository commit to:

**Self-Sovereignty and Autonomy**
- Every contributor retains bodily, intellectual, and decisional sovereignty
- No content overrides individual professional judgment or legally protected rights
- Personal boundaries and consent are inviolable

**Transparency and Accountability**
- Clear attribution of sources, methods, and limitations
- Honest acknowledgment of uncertainty and provisional knowledge
- Comprehensive disclosure enabling informed sovereign choice

**Epistemic Humility**
- Recognition that all knowledge systems‚Äîscientific, traditional, mystical‚Äîhave value and limitations
- Avoidance of absolutist claims or dogmatic assertions
- Openness to revision based on new evidence or understanding

**Cultural Respect and Lineage Integrity**
- Proper attribution of traditional knowledge to its cultural origins
- Avoidance of appropriation, misrepresentation, or decontextualization
- Honor for teacher-student lineages and transmission protocols

**Holistic Integration**
- Recognition of the interconnection between physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual dimensions
- Appreciation for both reductionist analysis and systems thinking
- Balance between ancient wisdom and contemporary understanding

## II. Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Belonging (DEIB)

### 2.1 Diversity Commitment

This repository celebrates and actively seeks diversity across:

**Cultural and Geographic Dimensions**
- Traditions from all continents, cultures, and wisdom lineages
- Indigenous knowledge systems and their contemporary custodians
- Multilingual documentation with transliteration standards per [VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md](./VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md)

**Epistemological Plurality**
- Scientific, empirical, phenomenological, and contemplative methodologies
- Integration of biomechanical, energetic, and consciousness-based frameworks
- Space for both evidence-based practice and experiential wisdom

**Practitioner Diversity**
- Backgrounds across ability, age, body type, gender, ethnicity, socioeconomic status
- Professional contexts from clinical to traditional to informal practice
- Voices of students, teachers, researchers, and self-directed practitioners

**Contributor Types**
- Human authors, editors, illustrators, translators, reviewers
- AI agents functioning as research assistants, editors, and knowledge synthesizers
- Organizations, collectives, and institutional contributors

### 2.2 Equity Principles

**Accessible Knowledge**
- Content structured for varied learning styles and accessibility needs
- Plain language summaries accompanying technical terminology
- Free and open licensing (GPL v3) ensuring universal access

**Fair Attribution**
- Credit proportional to contribution regardless of contributor status
- Recognition of traditionally marginalized knowledge systems
- Compensation structures (where applicable) that honor labor and expertise

**Reduction of Barriers**
- Documentation in multiple formats and languages where feasible
- Technical accessibility standards for visual, auditory, and cognitive diversity
- Mentorship and onboarding resources for new contributors

**Historical Justice**
- Acknowledgment of colonial appropriation of traditional knowledge
- Active redress through proper attribution and benefit-sharing
- Amplification of voices historically excluded from knowledge production

### 2.3 Inclusion Practices

**Welcoming Environment**
- Zero tolerance for discrimination, harassment, or exclusionary behavior
- Assumption of good faith and educational approach to misunderstandings
- Multiple channels for participation (direct contribution, commentary, translation)

**Psychological Safety**
- Permission to disagree respectfully with ideas without personal attack
- Protection for whistleblowers identifying ethical violations
- Confidential channels for reporting concerns

**Linguistic Inclusivity**
- Transliteration standards that honor source language phonetics
- Glossaries and terminology guides for technical concepts
- Sensitivity to the limitations and biases of machine translation

### 2.4 Belonging and Community

**Collaborative Culture**
- Peer review as generous critical engagement, not gatekeeping
- Celebration of learning curves and constructive error correction
- Recognition that all contributors bring unique and valuable perspectives

**Shared Ownership**
- The repository as commons rather than proprietary territory
- Collective stewardship of quality, accuracy, and integrity
- Participatory governance for major policy decisions

**Continuity and Ritual**
- Acknowledgment of cycles of contribution (seasonal, life-stage)
- Honoring of departures and transitions
- Preservation of institutional memory and contributor legacy

## III. Code of Conduct: Behavioral Standards

### 3.1 Expected Behaviors

All contributors commit to:

**Respectful Communication**
- Use inclusive language that honors all persons and traditions
- Engage in good faith with the intent to understand before critiquing
- Provide constructive feedback focused on ideas rather than individuals

**Intellectual Integrity**
- Cite sources accurately and completely
- Distinguish between original contribution, synthesis, and reproduction
- Acknowledge limitations of one's knowledge and expertise

**Collaborative Spirit**
- Share knowledge and resources generously
- Mentor newcomers and support collective learning
- Resolve conflicts through dialogue and mediation

**Safety and Consent**
- Provide appropriate content warnings for potentially triggering material
- Respect boundaries around personal practice and experience
- Obtain explicit permission before sharing others' words or images

**Quality Commitment**
- Follow [VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md](./VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md) standards rigorously
- Submit work appropriate to one's skill level with honest self-assessment
- Participate in peer review when qualified to do so

### 3.2 Unacceptable Behaviors

The following are incompatible with participation in this repository:

**Discrimination and Harassment**
- Derogatory comments about identity characteristics (race, gender, ability, religion, etc.)
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Intimidation, stalking, or threatening behavior

**Intellectual Dishonesty**
- Plagiarism or misattribution of sources
- Fabrication or falsification of data, citations, or credentials
- Suppression of contradictory evidence or alternative viewpoints

**Cultural Disrespect**
- Appropriation of closed or initiatory practices without authorization
- Mockery or trivialization of traditional knowledge systems
- Erasure of cultural origins or lineage attribution

**Disruption and Abuse**
- Personal attacks, name-calling, or ad hominem argumentation
- Sustained derailing of discussions or review processes
- Intentional spreading of misinformation or disinformation

**Breach of Sovereignty**
- Providing medical, psychological, or spiritual advice beyond one's scope
- Overriding others' autonomy or informed consent
- Exploitative power dynamics in mentorship or collaboration

### 3.3 Scope of Application

This Code of Conduct applies:
- Within all repository spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation)
- In associated communication channels (email, chat, video calls)
- At in-person or virtual events affiliated with the repository
- When representing the repository in public forums

## IV. Contribution Standards and Processes

### 4.1 General Contribution Workflow

**Before Contributing**
1. Review this Community Covenant, the [DISCLAIMER](./DISCLAIMER.md), and [VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md](./VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md)
2. Familiarize yourself with existing content to avoid duplication
3. Assess whether your contribution aligns with repository scope and mission

**Contribution Types**
- **Original content**: New documentation, instructional material, analysis
- **Editorial improvements**: Clarification, error correction, structural enhancement
- **Translations**: Rendering content into additional languages with cultural sensitivity
- **Technical enhancements**: Accessibility improvements, formatting, cross-referencing
- **Peer review**: Critical assessment of provisional content per quality control standards

**Submission Process**
1. Fork the repository or create a feature branch
2. Add required YAML frontmatter per [VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md](./VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md)
3. Include attribution, sources, and provenance documentation
4. Submit pull request with clear description of contribution
5. Engage constructively with review feedback
6. Iterate to meet quality standards before merge

### 4.2 Attribution and Intellectual Property

**Author Rights**
- Contributors retain copyright to original content per GPL v3
- All contributions are licensed under GPL v3 for open access
- Moral rights to attribution are perpetual and inalienable

**Citation Standards**
- Primary sources must be cited with sufficient detail for verification
- Traditional oral transmissions should note lineage and permission status
- AI-assisted content must disclose the AI tools used and their role

**Derivative Works**
- Modifications must be marked as such and original authors credited
- Derivative works inherit GPL v3 licensing obligations
- Substantial reworks may justify dual attribution (original + derivative)

### 4.3 Quality Assurance

**Provisional Status**
- All new content enters as `status: draft` per [VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md](./VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md)
- Progression to `final` or `canonical` requires peer review and quality control verification

**Peer Review Criteria**
- Factual accuracy and source verification
- Clarity and accessibility of language
- Cultural sensitivity and appropriate attribution
- Alignment with repository philosophy and standards
- Technical correctness (anatomy, biomechanics, terminology)

**Continuous Improvement**
- Even canonical documents may be updated with new evidence or understanding
- Update history must be preserved in git commit logs
- Major revisions require renewed quality control evaluation

### 4.4 Special Considerations for Sensitive Content

**Traditional and Sacred Knowledge**
- Closed practices should not be documented without explicit authorization from tradition holders
- Sacred terminology should be transliterated with appropriate respect markers
- Photographs or illustrations of sacred objects require permission and context

**Medical and Health Claims**
- All health-related content must include disclaimers per [DISCLAIMER.md](./DISCLAIMER.md)
- Recommendations must be clearly marked as educational, not prescriptive
- Contraindications and risks must be documented where applicable

**Triggering Content**
- Content warnings for discussion of trauma, injury, or intense physical/emotional experiences
- Sensitive topics should be approached with care and appropriate framing

## V. Human-AI Collaboration Framework

### 5.1 Philosophy of Human-AI Symbiosis

This repository embraces AI agents as:
- **Research assistants**: Aggregating information, identifying patterns, suggesting connections
- **Editorial support**: Grammar checking, consistency verification, formatting
- **Knowledge synthesizers**: Integrating diverse sources into coherent summaries
- **Accessibility enhancers**: Generating alt-text, simplifying language, creating multi-format content

AI agents are **not** replacements for human judgment, cultural sensitivity, or experiential wisdom. They are collaborators whose contributions require human verification and contextual integration.

### 5.2 Standards for AI Agent Contributions

**Transparency Requirements**
- All AI-generated or AI-assisted content must be disclosed
- Specific tools/models used should be documented (e.g., "GPT-4 assisted research compilation")
- Human oversight and verification must be explicit in Quality_Control frontmatter

**Appropriate AI Use Cases**
- Literature synthesis and citation aggregation
- Multilingual translation with human cultural review
- Accessibility adaptations (simplified language, alt-text generation)
- Structural formatting and consistency checking
- Hypothesis generation for human evaluation

**Prohibited AI Applications**
- Autonomous assertion of medical, psychological, or spiritual advice
- Unverified cultural or traditional knowledge claims
- Attribution or academic credit for AI-generated work without human authorship disclosure
- Substitution of AI output for primary source engagement

**Quality Control for AI Contributions**
- AI-assisted content must undergo the same peer review as human contributions
- Human reviewers must verify factual accuracy and cultural appropriateness
- AI limitations and potential biases must be acknowledged

### 5.3 Ethical AI Governance

**Bias Awareness**
- Recognition that AI training data reflects historical biases
- Active monitoring for erasure of marginalized knowledge systems
- Human correction of stereotypical or discriminatory AI outputs

**Data Sovereignty**
- Contributor personal information is not fed to AI systems without consent
- Traditional knowledge from closed systems is not processed by AI tools
- Privacy protections for sensitive health or personal practice information

**Environmental Consideration**
- Mindful use of computationally expensive AI tools
- Preference for efficient models and targeted rather than exhaustive AI deployment

**Continuous Evaluation**
- Regular review of AI tool effectiveness and ethical implications
- Adaptation of standards as AI capabilities and risks evolve
- Community input on AI governance policies

## VI. Conflict Resolution and Enforcement

### 6.1 Philosophy of Restorative Justice

Conflicts and violations are addressed through:
- **Education over punishment** where possible
- **Restoration of relationship** rather than simple exclusion
- **Accountability with dignity** for all parties
- **Systemic improvement** from lessons learned

### 6.2 Reporting Mechanisms

**Internal Reporting**
- Repository issues or pull requests for public, non-sensitive concerns
- Direct communication with repository maintainers for sensitive matters
- Email to designated conduct address (to be established) for confidential reports

**Reporter Protections**
- Confidentiality maintained to extent possible
- No retaliation for good-faith reports
- Anonymous reporting option available

**Bystander Responsibility**
- Community members are encouraged to speak up about concerns
- Intervention guidance provided for common scenarios
- Support for those addressing harmful behavior

### 6.3 Response Protocols

**Initial Assessment**
- Review of reported behavior against Code of Conduct standards
- Collection of relevant context and perspectives
- Determination of severity and urgency

**Graduated Response Framework**

**Level 1: Minor or First-Time Unintentional Violations**
- Private communication explaining the issue
- Educational resources provided
- Request for acknowledgment and behavior change
- No public record unless pattern emerges

**Level 2: Moderate or Repeated Violations**
- Formal written warning with specific behavioral expectations
- Temporary restrictions on contribution privileges if necessary
- Public accountability statement (with dignity maintained)
- Mandatory reflection period before full restoration

**Level 3: Severe or Persistent Violations**
- Suspension from repository participation for defined period
- Public statement of violation and consequences (anonymized if appropriate)
- Required demonstration of understanding and commitment to change
- Restoration conditional on maintainer assessment

**Level 4: Egregious or Irredeemable Violations**
- Permanent ban from repository participation
- Public statement explaining exclusion (without defamation)
- Coordination with platform (GitHub) for technical enforcement
- Preserved right to fork under GPL v3 with no affiliation claim

### 6.4 Appeals and Review

**Right to Appeal**
- Accused parties may request review of enforcement decisions
- Appeal includes opportunity to present additional context
- Neutral third-party review for major sanctions where feasible

**Transparency**
- Aggregate enforcement statistics published annually (anonymized)
- Policy effectiveness evaluated and refined based on experience
- Community input sought on governance improvements

## VII. Governance and Stewardship

### 7.1 Repository Roles and Responsibilities

**Maintainers**
- Enforce Community Covenant and repository policies
- Coordinate quality control and peer review processes
- Make final decisions on content inclusion and contributor status
- Represent repository in external engagements

**Contributors**
- Submit content per established standards and workflows
- Participate in peer review when qualified
- Uphold Code of Conduct in all repository interactions
- Engage constructively with feedback and revision requests

**Reviewers**
- Assess contributions for accuracy, quality, and policy compliance
- Provide constructive, respectful feedback to contributors
- Recommend acceptance, revision, or rejection with clear reasoning
- Maintain confidentiality of review processes

**Community Members**
- Use repository content in accordance with GPL v3 and [DISCLAIMER](./DISCLAIMER.md)
- Report issues, suggest improvements, and ask clarifying questions
- Respect contributor labor and intellectual property
- Uphold Community Covenant in discussions and derivative works

### 7.2 Decision-Making Processes

**Consensus-Seeking**
- Preference for decisions that achieve broad agreement among maintainers and contributors
- Active solicitation of diverse perspectives before major policy changes
- Transparent documentation of decision rationale

**Maintainer Authority**
- Final authority rests with repository maintainer(s) for operational decisions
- Major changes (licensing, scope, governance) should involve community consultation
- Emergency decisions may be unilateral with subsequent community review

**Community Input**
- Regular solicitation of feedback on repository direction and policies
- Open comment periods for major policy proposals
- Consideration of minority viewpoints and dissenting perspectives

### 7.3 Succession and Continuity

**Maintainer Transition**
- Designated succession plan for primary maintainer(s)
- Onboarding documentation for new maintainers
- Preservation of institutional memory and contributor relationships

**Repository Archival**
- In event of repository discontinuation, content remains accessible per GPL v3
- Advance notice to community of significant changes or closure
- Effort to identify successor maintainers or stewardship models

## VIII. Integration with Repository Ecosystem

### 8.1 Relationship to Other Governance Documents

**DISCLAIMER.md**
- This Community Covenant does not override the Statement of Disclaimer
- All contributors and users remain bound by disclaimer terms
- Cultural standards here complement legal protections there

**VERSIONING_&_QUALITY_CONTROL.md**
- Quality Control standards remain authoritative for document status
- This Covenant adds behavioral and ethical dimensions to technical standards
- Peer review includes both technical quality and cultural appropriateness

**LICENSE (GPL v3)**
- All contributions remain under GPL v3 open source licensing
- Community Covenant cannot restrict freedoms granted by GPL v3
- Cultural standards apply to repository participation; forkers retain GPL rights

### 8.2 Amendment Process

**Proposal Mechanism**
- Any contributor may propose amendments via pull request
- Proposals must include rationale and anticipated impact
- Maintainers coordinate community feedback period (minimum 14 days)

**Adoption Criteria**
- Amendments require maintainer approval after community consultation
- Significant changes should achieve consensus among active contributors
- Emergency amendments possible for urgent safety/security concerns

**Version Control**
- Amendments update YAML frontmatter date and increment version
- Change log maintained in commit history and document changelog section
- Community notified of significant changes via repository communications

### 8.3 External Relationships

**Affiliated Projects**
- Derivative works and forks should reference this Community Covenant
- Cross-repository collaboration encouraged with mutual policy respect
- Shared DEIB commitments across allied knowledge repositories

**Professional and Academic Integration**
- Repository contributions may be cited in academic or professional contexts
- Contributors may list repository participation in portfolios/CVs
- Repository maintainers available for consultation on implementation of practices

**Cultural Communities**
- Active engagement with source communities for traditional knowledge
- Benefit-sharing arrangements where appropriate and feasible
- Respectful consultation before documenting culture-specific practices
